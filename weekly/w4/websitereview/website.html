Geek Culture Website Review

estarland.com

This is the main website for a popular video game store in Chantilly. The homepage clearly gives off a vibe of geek culture, informing the

user about new releases and other deals they have to offer. It's not too flashy and has enough detail to make it not seem too basic.

GameStop

Similarly to eStarLand's website, the homepage is easy and simple to understand and navigate while also informing the viewer of any new

releases. This also includes new deals, a staple in most online video game marketplaces.

Metacritic

A rather basic design which still gets the job done, Metacritic has an excellent search system for multiple video games, shows and

movies that have been reviewed over the years. Everything you need to use to search is conveniently placed on the top of the screen,

above more news about the latest ratings the website's top reviewers have posted.

NintendoLife

This news site focused solely on Nintendo products has a Nintendo feel to it, with its red and black borders and Mario patterned background.

While this site isn't owned by Nintendo per se, it still shows that this website is dedicated to help spread the news about whatever Nintendo

has planned soon.

Katsucon

Everything needed to look up info or get registered for this Japanese convention is all placed together on the top of the homepage, neatly

organized above a scrolling slideshow of other exhibits and activities the convention has to offer.
